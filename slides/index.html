Concurrency isn't Parallelism

  • Concurrency - multiple things take place over the same time frame
  • Parallelism - multiple things happening in the same literal instant of time

Hardware is easier than software (sorta)

  • We are *good* at making multicore chips
  • We are *bad* making use of those cores in software
  • Software must get better at concurrency in general
  • Interpreters and languages in general must offer genuine parallelism

Threads

  • Fundamental abstraction of schedulable computational execution
  • Interpreters have threads
  • Operating systems have threads

But what kind of thread does my interpreter use?

GIL or no GIL

Threading in Ruby comes in two basic flavors -- those VMs that have a Global Interpreter Lock(GIL) and those that don't.

  • MRI 1.8x - N Ruby VM threads GIL'd to 1 OS thread
  • MRI 1.9x - N Ruby VM threads GIL'd to N OS threads
  • JRuby and Rubinius (2.0+) - VM threads correspond directly to OS threads
